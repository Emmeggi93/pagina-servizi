Amalfi Coast

For Amalfi Coast we mean Amalfi, Positano, Praiano, Ravello. It is quite impossible to visit them all in one day, so maybe pick max 3 of them (my personal top 3 is Positano, Ravello and Amalfi).

These villages are all on the same road, one after the other, and there is no train station. So the only ways to get there are by boat or by road.

By Road:
Buses: they leave from Sorrento train station, ticket is not expensive but the road is very winding, so choose this if you don't usually get sick.

Pompeii

If you are asking how to go to Pompeii, we definitely say "by train".

From Sorrento train station to Pompeii it is just 40 minutes, the ticket is around 4â‚¬ and the stop is right in front of the gate of the ruins.

It is possible to buy the ticket online through the TicketOne circuit (official seller). It is also possible to buy the ticket at the entrances and at the self-service ticket offices.

Pompeii is a proper city largely preserved under the ash, frozen at the moment it was buried. Now it is a very large site to discover, you could visit it multiple times and not yet see everything.

For this reason we suggest you to go there for more than just one/two hours and be part of a guided tour, or at least rent audio guides at the ticket desk.

Guided tours can be booked at the Information desk in Piazza Esedra and Piazza Anfiteatro entrance.
